03 · Research

Interdisciplinary Projects

Integrating engineering science, data tools, and sustainability thinking into meaningful, real-world research.

Project 01 · Non-Destructive Testing

UPV Testing of Concrete Quality

Structural Assessment

Objective

Evaluate internal concrete quality non-destructively using Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity testing — without compromising structural integrity.

Methodology

  • Conducted pulse transmission tests on concrete specimens
  • Compared UPV readings against standard strength parameters
  • Used Excel for systematic data interpretation and tabulation

Outcome

Developed deep understanding of NDT methods for real-world infrastructure assessment.

✓ Foundation for Automation Tool (Project 3)
Project 02 · Sustainability Research

Life Cycle Assessment of Concrete Materials

Environmental Impact

Objective

Compare the full environmental footprint of conventional concrete against sustainable and supplementary cementitious material alternatives.

Methodology

  • Carbon footprint estimation across production, use & end-of-life stages
  • Material lifecycle comparison using sustainability metrics
  • Embodied energy and CO₂ emission differential analysis

Outcome

Identified eco-friendly alternatives with measurably lower environmental impact — directly informing real sustainable practice.

✓ Published findings shared with IGBC chapter

07 · Global Awareness

Ethics & Global Responsibility

🌿 Green Building Practice

IGBC AP certification and chapter leadership give me a rigorous framework for responsible design, material use, and energy efficiency.

♿ Inclusive Engineering

Deed Mobility Solutions is a direct commitment — engineering must serve the most vulnerable members of society, not just the most profitable markets.

🌍 Carbon & Lifecycle Awareness

My LCA research revealed that construction decisions today have generational environmental consequences — shaping every design choice I make.

⚖️ Profit vs. Public Welfare

Infrastructure must balance commercial viability with community wellbeing, safety, and long-term environmental stewardship.

As a future construction project manager, I believe we are stewards of the built environment — every structure we design or manage will outlast us by decades, if not centuries. That responsibility demands we think beyond cost and schedule to ask: who does this serve, and at what cost to the world?

My IGBC AP certification and chapter leadership gave me a rigorous framework for green building thinking — from responsible material selection to energy-efficient design and long-term environmental impact. This isn't just theory; I've actively promoted it through workshops, expert talks, and real construction site visits.

Co-founding Deed Mobility Solutions taught me that the most ethical engineering often means doing what no one else is doing — building an accessible electric vehicle for people with disabilities isn't a profitable mass market. It's the right thing to build. That distinction matters deeply to me.

I aspire to lead projects that are structurally sound and delivered on time — but also built with integrity, serving their communities, and leaving the environment better than we found it.
